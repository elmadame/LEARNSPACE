1. What is a Process? A series of activities that transforms inputs into outputs.
2. Galloway's Definition: A process is a sequence of steps that converts inputs to outputs.
3. Juran's Definition: Systematic actions aimed at achieving pre-defined objectives.
4. ISO 9000: A process transforms elements into results through interconnected activities.
5. Business Process Management (BPM): A structured approach to optimizing business processes.
6. Kilmann (1995): BPM helps companies maintain a competitive edge.
7. Five Phases of BPM: Identification, Design, Implementation, Review, and Improvement.
8. Identification Phase: Identify key processes critical to achieving goals.
9. Process Mapping: A visual representation of who does what and when in a process.
10. IDEF0 Methodology: A process modeling tool for understanding interactions between processes.
